  New file: templates/terms.html

  What it contains:
  - Extends base.html so it inherits the navbar, footer, and styling
  - Sets a custom page title: "Terms and Conditions — Spendly"
  - A terms-section div with the main content
  - Standard legal sections:
    - Acceptance of Terms — statement that using the app means you agree

    - User Responsibilities — you're responsible for your account security
    - Limitations of Liability — standard "as-is" disclaimer
    - Changes to Terms — we can update these anytime
    - Governing Law — legal jurisdiction
    - Contact Us — how to reach support

  All sections are generic and appropriate for a personal expense tracker.

  ---
  3. Update base.html footer link

  Location: In the footer, the "Terms and Conditions" link (currently line 39)

  Current:
  <a href="#">Terms and Conditions</a>

  Updated to:
  <a href="{{ url_for('terms') }}">Terms and Conditions</a>

  Why:
  - {{ url_for('terms') }} is a Flask template function that generates the
  correct URL for the terms() route
  - When clicked, it will navigate to /terms instead of staying on the same page
  - If the route ever changes, this automatically updates (no hardcoded /terms
  URL)
